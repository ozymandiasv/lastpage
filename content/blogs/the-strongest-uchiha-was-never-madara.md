A lot of Naruto fans assume that Madara Uchiha was the strongest Uchiha to ever exist and that nobody could realistically challenge him until Six Paths Rinnegan Sasuke Uchiha arrived during the Fourth Shinobi World War. To be fair, that opinion is not unreasonable. War Arc Madara was ridiculous. By the time he acquired the Rinnegan, Limbo clones, Six Paths chakra, and enough plot armor to survive an entire alliance throwing everything at him, he had become less of a character and more of a natural disaster. Defeating him stopped feeling like a battle and started feeling like a scheduling issue for Masashi Kishimoto. But if we strip away all the war arc madness and focus only on the abilities that naturally belonged to the Uchiha clan, I think there is another candidate who deserves far more attention than he receives.
And that candidate is Obito Uchiha.
## The Eye That Never Existed
The biggest problem with discussing Obito's potential is that we never actually got to see it. For most of the story, Obito is operating with a handicap that fans rarely acknowledge. He never possessed both of his Mangekyō Sharingan for any meaningful period of his life. One eye belonged to Kakashi Hatake, while the other remained with Obito. As a result, fans often compare a complete Madara against an incomplete Obito, which feels slightly unfair. Imagine judging Sasuke Uchiha while removing one of his eyes for most of the series.
The hypothetical becomes fascinating when we ask a simple question. What if the accident never happened? What if Obito retained both eyes, awakened a complete Dual Mangekyō Sharingan, and eventually developed an Eternal Mangekyō Sharingan? Suddenly we are not talking about the Obito we know. We are talking about a completely different monster.
## Kamui Was Already Broken
The reason I lean toward Obito is simple. Kamui is arguably the most broken Mangekyō Sharingan ability in the entire franchise. Even Kakashi's version of Kamui was absurdly powerful despite his limited chakra reserves and the fact that he was not a natural Uchiha. The ability could erase attacks, remove opponents from the battlefield, manipulate space itself, and effectively bypass conventional combat. Obito's version was even worse because it granted near-perfect intangibility. Entire armies, elite shinobi, and Kage-level opponents struggled to land a single meaningful hit on him.
Now imagine both Kamui abilities existing within a single user.
People often forget that Dual Mangekyō Sharingan users can access Susanoo. If Obito possessed both eyes, he would almost certainly have unlocked his own complete Susanoo decades before the war. We briefly witnessed what a fraction of that potential looked like when Kakashi temporarily received Obito's chakra and unlocked a Kamui-enhanced Susanoo. That version was powerful enough to challenge god-tier opponents. A lifelong Dual Mangekyō Sharingan Obito mastering those abilities from childhood onward would have been terrifying.
## The Boy Who Fought a Hokage
One of the most underrated feats in Naruto belongs to a teenage Obito. At an age when most shinobi were still trying to establish themselves, Obito infiltrated Konoha, controlled the Nine-Tailed Fox, and fought Minato Namikaze, arguably the strongest Hokage of his generation. Yes, Minato won. Nobody disputes that. But context matters.
This was not a fully realized Obito.
This was a teenager.
A teenager who had only recently awakened his abilities.
A teenager who pushed the Yellow Flash to the absolute limit.
Imagine that same Obito possessing both Mangekyō Sharingan and years of combat experience. The fight becomes much less one-sided. In fact, one could reasonably argue that a mature Dual Mangekyō Sharingan Obito would eventually surpass Minato entirely. The fact that such discussions are even possible tells you how absurd his potential was.
## The Gifted Child Behind the Mask
Madara was a genius. Sasuke was a genius. Itachi Uchiha was perhaps the greatest prodigy the clan ever produced. Yet there is something uniquely terrifying about Obito's growth curve. Unlike the others, much of his potential remained theoretical. We are judging him based on what he accomplished while incomplete.
Even with one eye, he manipulated the entire shinobi world from the shadows. He controlled Akatsuki, challenged multiple Kage, participated in the Fourth Shinobi World War, and remained the only dangerous individuals alive. Most characters become weaker after losing half their primary abilities. Obito somehow became one of the strongest characters in the series despite it.
That alone says everything.
## The Only Person Who Could Defeat Obito
The irony of Obito's story is that he was never truly defeated because someone overpowered him. He was defeated because Kakashi existed.
Kamui's greatest strength was also its greatest weakness. The only person who possessed intimate knowledge of the ability was the man carrying Obito's other eye. Without Kakashi, many of the strategies used against Obito simply do not exist. The alliance could not touch him. Most Kage could not touch him. Even Might Guy's Eighth Gate presents a strange problem because raw power means very little if you cannot land a hit.
This is why I have always appreciated the way Kishimoto handled Obito's downfall. It was not a case of introducing a stronger character to solve the problem. It was a case of creating a weakness years before the audience understood why it mattered. Obito was ultimately defeated by the one person who understood his power better than anyone else because he literally possessed half of it.
For all the debates about Madara, Sasuke, and Itachi, I keep coming back to the same conclusion. If Obito had retained both eyes and naturally developed into adulthood without losing half of his greatest ability, Naruto may have accidentally created the strongest Uchiha who ever lived. Not the strongest by war arc power scaling, not the strongest because of borrowed god powers, but the strongest by virtue of his own abilities. And the most terrifying part is that we never even got to see what that version of Obito looked like.
