The Enforcement Directorate arrived at Pinarayi Vijayan's house at seven in the morning. The very next morning after the Kerala High Court refused to quash the case or stop the investigation. Not a week later. Not after some deliberation. The next morning. At 7.
I want you to sit with that timing for a second.
Our investigative agencies, the ones that have spent years moving at the pace of institutions that know they will never be held accountable, suddenly remembered how to set an alarm. Suddenly remembered that they had places to be, doors to knock on, a former Chief Minister to raid. The efficiency is impressive. The timing is not a coincidence and everyone knows it is not a coincidence and nobody in power will say it is not a coincidence.
Now. Is Pinarayi Vijayan corrupt? I genuinely do not know. He is someone I respect for specific reasons and disrespect for other specific reasons, and I have never made the mistake of thinking those two things cannot coexist in the same person. If he is corrupt, he should be tried. That part is simple. What is not simple is the instrument being used to try him.
## The Weapon They Named a Law
The ED is acting under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act. I have said this before and I will keep saying it until it stops being true. The PMLA is not functioning as a law. It is functioning as a weapon. A clean, legally dressed, constitutionally difficult to challenge weapon that the ruling party has aimed, with remarkable consistency, at whoever is currently inconvenient.
The pattern is not subtle. Opposition leader gets targeted by ED. Charges pile up. Then, quietly, they cross the floor. They join the BJP. And the charges disappear. Not dismissed after trial. Not resolved through any process resembling justice. Just gone. Because the weapon was never about the crime. It was about the calculation.
What I want, and what I think is the only reasonable position here, is not the abolition of PMLA. It is a fast track court. A trial within 90 days. Bail functioning the way bail is supposed to function in a criminal case. The law should not allow a person to be held without trial indefinitely. That is not a legal system. That is a cage with paperwork.
And then there is Umar Khalid. Five years. No trial. Under UAPA, which makes PMLA look almost reasonable by comparison. If the state has evidence that someone is a threat to the nation, produce it in court and prove it. That is the entire arrangement. That is what separates a democracy from a regime that has simply learned to use longer words. Keeping a person in prison for five years without establishing guilt is not caution. It is cruelty with a legal address.
## Congress Said Nothing
This is the part that genuinely surprised me and also, on reflection, did not surprise me at all.
Pinarayi Vijayan stood up when Rahul Gandhi was summoned by the ED. Called it an attack on democracy. Was loud about it, public about it, consistent about it. And now the ED is at his door at seven in the morning and Congress has gone very quiet.
I have been trying to find the right image for what Congress is doing and I think I found it. They are playing the Allies while BJP and the Left bleed each other out. Letting Hitler and Stalin exhaust one another, watching the destruction from a careful distance, and planning to walk into whatever remains when both sides have spent themselves. That is the Congress strategy.  Positioning and let the fight happen, stay clean, inherit the ruins.
I would not be surprised, and I mean this without dramatics, if Congress finds a quiet alignment with BJP before this decade ends. Not formally. Not with a press conference. But in the way that actually matters, which is in the silences they keep choosing at exactly the moments when speaking would cost them something.
Pinarayi raised his voice for them. They could not find theirs for him.
The raid happened at seven in the morning. Congress slept through it. And somewhere in that image is everything you need to know about where Indian democracy is being quietly taken while the rest of us are still arguing about who deserves it.
