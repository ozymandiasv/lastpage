Nolan has always been fascinated by men who build something they cannot control, and Oppenheimer is the purest distillation of that obsession yet. It is not really a war film. It is a courtroom drama disguised as a biopic, cutting between the desert at Los Alamos and a sterile security hearing years later, where the man who gave America the bomb is quietly dismantled by the country he gave it to.

## The Weight of the Non-Linear

The fractured timeline could easily have felt like a gimmick, but it works because the film is not really about the bomb going off. It is about the moment right after, the one nobody plans for, when the thing you built becomes real and you have to live in the world it made. Cillian Murphy plays that reckoning with a stillness that says more than any monologue could.

## Verdict

Not an easy watch, and not meant to be one. But it is the rare blockbuster that trusts its audience to sit with discomfort instead of resolving it.
