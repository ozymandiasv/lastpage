---
title: "Running Man"
type: "Verse"
date: "2026-05-29"
published: true
---

His alarm rings at five while the streetlights are still buzzing,
breaking his back all day just to hand the check to the bank,
borrowing from Peter today so Paul stays off the porch tomorrow.
A stray dog sleeps beneath the steps without paying rent for the dirt,
a bird pulls a worm from the earth and owes nobody a thing,
but a man grinds his hands to the bone for the right to eat,
for the right to sleep, for the right to wake up and do it again,
trapped in a cage built from interest rates, mortgages, and overdue notices,
watching every hour of his life auctioned off before he even lives it,
while the animals roam the world as they were born to,
and the only creature that has to earn the right to exist
is the one foolish enough to call itself free.
