The Day I Realized I Hate Arsenal

Just a club whose defeats seem to bring me an unreasonable amount of joy.

I didn’t know I hated Arsenal until I watched the Champions League final against Paris Saint-Germain. Even writing that sentence feels strange because I had never consciously thought of Arsenal as a club I disliked. I am a Liverpool fan. My football emotions are usually reserved for Liverpool’s victories and defeats. If anything, logic says I should dislike Manchester United more. Maybe Everton. Maybe even Tottenham. Arsenal never really belonged on that list. Yet somewhere over the years, they quietly found a place there without me ever noticing. When Arsenal won the Premier League this year, I wasn’t particularly upset. Football becomes boring when the same clubs keep winning everything. Seeing somebody else lift the trophy every now and then is good for the league. I looked at Arsenal winning it and thought, fair enough. They’ve been knocking on the door for years. They probably deserved one. But the Champions League final felt completely different. Finals have a strange way of exposing emotions you didn’t even know you had. Before the match kicked off, I found myself supporting PSG, which is ridiculous because I don’t particularly like PSG either. I have a lot of respect for Luis Enrique and what he has built there, but PSG as a club has never been easy to like. The whole Messi, Neymar, and Mbappé era always felt less like building a football team and more like somebody trying to assemble the Avengers with unlimited money. Yet there I was, hoping they would win, not because I wanted PSG to become champions, but because I absolutely did not want Arsenal becoming European champions. That was the moment I realised something had gone horribly wrong. Then came the penalties. Of all the ways Arsenal could lose, penalties felt like the least likely one. This is a team that has turned set pieces into an entire industry under Mikel Arteta. Every corner feels rehearsed. Every dead-ball situation feels like a planned operation. You almost expect them to score before the ball is even crossed. So naturally, football decided to be football. The match drifted into the one situation where Arsenal should have felt comfortable, and somehow they collapsed anyway. Watching Gabriel Jesus miss his penalty genuinely felt like football had decided to tell a joke, and for reasons I still can’t explain, I enjoyed every second of it. The strange part is that I still don’t know why. Maybe every football fan has one completely irrational dislike. A club that annoys them for reasons they can’t properly explain. A team whose defeats somehow feel more satisfying than they probably should. Arsenal might simply be mine. I don’t have a grand theory. I don’t have a long list of complaints. I don’t even dislike most of their players. All I know is that when the final whistle blew, I celebrated a little harder than any neutral observer had any right to. And then I realised something else. Arsenal may have won European trophies before, but somehow they’ve still never managed to get their hands on the one that matters most. Honestly, that’s probably the funniest part.
