If art is supposed to influence culture, as the Romantics believed, then art is superior and should influence morals. However, I think art has no such commitments; it is as plain as Walter Pater's version: art for art's sake. We can allow the coexistence of Breaking Bad, The Boys, The Pursuit of Happyness, and Dead Poets Society. Propaganda, however, is where art begs to differ. But doesn't that also mean art influences society? So, was Walter wrong and Coleridge and Shelley right, or is it the typical chicken-and-egg paradox? Art does not have a mandate to influence morals, but society inevitably absorbs morals and values from the art it consumes. So does that make art obligatory to uplift morals?
