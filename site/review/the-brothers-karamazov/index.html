The Brothers Karamazov

Dostoevsky's last and largest novel is still the most complete argument for and against God ever written in fiction.

Type Book
Rating 10/10
Stars ★★★★★

Every few years I return to the Grand Inquisitor chapter alone, and every time it reads like it was written yesterday. Dostoevsky does not write villains, he writes positions, and he stress-tests every one of them against the others until you are no longer sure which brother he wants you to become.

Verdict

It is long, it is dense, and it will outlast every trend in fiction that comes after it. Read it once for the story, then again for everything underneath it.

Fyodor Dostoevsky Russian Literature Faith
